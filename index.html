geography
earth writing

cartography
science of mapmaking

map
two-dimensional or flat-scale model of Earth's surface or part of it
main purposes: as a reference or communications tools

map scale
relationship of a feature's size on a map to its actual size on Earth
-3 types
  • A ratio or fraction
  • numerical ratio, Ex. 1:24,000 or 1/24000
  • A written scale
  • words, Ex. "1 inch equals 1 mile"
  • A graphic scale
  • bar line
(first number refers to map distance, second number refers to distance on Earth's surface)

projection
scientific method of transferring locations on Earth's surface to a flat map
(because the Earth is spherical, flat maps have distortion)
-4 types of distortion:
  • shape of some areas appear more elongated or squat than reality
  • distance between two points may become increased or decreased
  • relative size of different areas at be altered, an area may appear larger than another, but is smaller in reality
  • direction from one place to another can be distorted
